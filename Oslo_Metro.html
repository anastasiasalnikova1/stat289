Oslo Metro

The Oslo Metro ( Norwegian : Oslo T-bane or Oslo Tunnelbane or simply T-banen ) is the rapid transit system of Oslo , Norway, operated by Sporveien T-banen on contract from the transit authority Ruter . The network consists of five lines that all run through the city centre, with a total length of 85 kilometres (53 mi), [2] serving 101 stations of which 17 are underground or indoors. In addition to serving 14 out of the 15 boroughs of Oslo (except St. Hanshaugen ), two lines run to Kolsås and Østerås , in the neighboring municipality of Bærum . In 2016, the system had an annual ridership of 118 million. [1]
