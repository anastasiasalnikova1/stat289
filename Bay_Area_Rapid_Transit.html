Bay Area Rapid Transit

Bay Area Rapid Transit ( BART ) is a rapid transit public transportation system serving the San Francisco Bay Area in California . The heavy rail elevated and subway system connects San Francisco and Oakland with urban and suburban areas in Alameda , Contra Costa , and San Mateo counties. BART serves 48 stations along six routes on 112 miles (180 km) of rapid transit lines, including a ten-mile spur line in eastern Contra Costa County which utilizes diesel multiple-unit trains and a 3.2-mile (5.1 km) automated guideway transit line to the Oakland International Airport . With an average of 423,000 weekday passengers and 124.2 million annual passengers in fiscal year 2017, [1] BART is the fifth-busiest heavy rail rapid transit system in the United States .

Bay Area Rapid Transit ( BART ) is a public transportation system serving the San Francisco Bay Area . The rapid transit elevated and subway system connects San Francisco with cities in the East Bay and the northern portion of San Mateo County . BART's rapid transit system operates 5 routes on 104 miles (167 km) of track, with 44 stations in 4 counties, which are Alameda County , Contra Costa County , City and County of San Francisco , and San Mateo County . Additionally, BART operates a 3.2-mile (5.1 km) AGT line to the Oakland International Airport which adds a 45th station to the system. A further spur line under BART jurisdiction in eastern Contra Costa County will utilize other rail technologies. With an average of 423,120 weekday passengers and 126 million annual passengers in fiscal year 2015, [1] BART is the fifth-busiest heavy rail rapid transit system in the United States . Growth in ridership slowed rapidly in 2016, [7] reflecting a national trend of declining use of public transit. [8] The name is spoken as an acronym instead of an initialism; it is referred to as "Bart," unlike "B-A-R-T."

BART (in full, San Francisco Bay Area Rapid Transit District ) is a rapid transit electric train service that serves parts of the San Francisco Bay Area , including the cities of San Francisco , Oakland , Berkeley , Fremont , and Walnut Creek . It also serves San Francisco International Airport directly via a station in the new International Terminal (Garage G/BART) and Oakland International Airport with connecting AirBART bus service. The trains are computer-controlled by a central computer located at BART's Lake Merritt station and headquarters, and generally arrive with regular punctuality. Train operators are present to make announcements, close doors, and operate the train in case of unforeseen difficulties. The BART acronym is pronounced as a single word, not as individual letters. In 2004 BART was named the #1 Transit System in America by the American Public Transportation Association . It beat out such systems as New York City’s MTA , the 2001 winner, and Denver’s Regional Transportation District , the 2003 winner.
