Algiers Metro

The Algiers Metro ( Arabic : مترو الجزائر العاصمة ‎, French : Métro d'Alger ), serving Algiers , the capital of Algeria , is a transport project dating from the 1970s that was designed to address the need for mass transport caused by the city's growth. Formally launched in the 1980s, the project slowed down due to financial difficulties and security issues in the 1990s. The project recommenced in 2003. Algerian President Abdelaziz Bouteflika attended the Metro's 31 October 2011 ribbon-cutting opening ceremony. [1] [2] The Algiers Metro then opened to passengers on the following day, 1 November 2011, [2] making Algiers only the second capital city in Africa (after Cairo ) to have a metro system. [6]
