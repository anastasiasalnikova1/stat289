Hangzhou Metro

Hangzhou Metro ( Chinese : 杭州地铁) is a metro system that serves the Chinese city of Hangzhou and surrounding suburbs. The system opened on November 24, 2012, [3] with major expansion plans still ongoing. It is the 17th city in China to operate a rapid transit system.

Template:Future chinese public transportation Hangzhou Metro ( Chinese : 杭州地铁) is a metro system currently under construction to serve the city of Hangzhou , Zhejiang and its suburbs. The construction commenced in March 2006 and its expected to complete by 2011 . Hangzhou is the tenth city in the People's Republic of China to have a rapid transit system in place. The first line, Line 1 is a 48 kilometer line with 30 stations. There is a total of 8 lines with a length of 278 km planned in the future. Line 1 will cost 22.08 billion yuan . Line 1 links Xiaoshan Xiang Lake , crosses the Jiantang River and ends in Xiasha and Linpin districts which links the city area to the suburban area of the city. [1]
