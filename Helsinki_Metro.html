Helsinki Metro

The Helsinki Metro ( Finnish : Helsingin metro , Swedish : Helsingfors metro ) is a rapid transit system serving Greater Helsinki , Finland . It is the world's northernmost metro system. The Helsinki Metro was opened to the general public on 2 August 1982 [3] after 27 years of planning. It is operated by Helsinki City Transport for HSL and carries 63 million passengers per year. [6]

The Helsinki Metro , or Helsingin metro in Finnish , is the metro system in Helsinki , Finland . It is the world's most northern metro. The system maintains a single forked line with 16 stations, out of which 8 are subterranean and 8 are surface stations. Trains travel either between Ruoholahti (Gräsviken) and Mellunmäki (Mellungsbacka) or between Ruoholahti and Vuosaari (Nordsjö), the tracks diverging at Itäkeskus (Östra Centrum).
