Cairo Metro

The Cairo Metro ( Arabic : مترو أنفاق القاهرة ‎, translit.   Metro Anfāq al-Qāhirah , lit. "Cairo Tunnel Metro" or مترو الأنفاق   pronounced  [ˈmetɾo lʔænˈfæːʔ] ) is the rapid transit system in Greater Cairo , Egypt . It was the first of only two full-fledged metro systems in Africa and only four in the Arab world . [8] [9] It was opened in 1987 as Line 1 from Helwan to Ramsis square with a length of 29 kilometres (18.0 mi). [10] As of 2014, the Cairo Metro has 61 stations (mostly At-grade), of which 3 are transfer stations, with a total length of 77.9 kilometres (48.4 mi). The system consists of three operational lines numbered from 1 to 3. As of 2013, the metro carried nearly 4 million passengers per day. [2]
