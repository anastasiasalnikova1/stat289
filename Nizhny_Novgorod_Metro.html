Nizhny Novgorod Metro

The Nizhny Novgorod Metro ( Russian : Нижегородское метро ), formerly known as the Gorky Metro ( Russian : Горьковское метро ), is a rapid-transit system which serves the city of Nizhny Novgorod , Russia . Opened in 1985, it consists of 15 stations [1] and is 18.8 kilometres (11.7 mi) long. [1] The metro connects with the City Rail system at the Moskovskaya station. It has the third-largest number of stations of any Russian subway system, the largest two being Moscow and St. Petersburg .

The Nizhny Novgorod Metro ( Russian : Нижегородский метрополитен ) is the third metro system built in Russia , located in Nizhny Novgorod (after Moscow and Saint Petersburg Metros ). Planning for the system began in the 1970's with the groundbreaking on December 17 , 1977 , on Leninskaya Station.
